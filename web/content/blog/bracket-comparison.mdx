March Madness is one of the most exciting events in sports, and the bracket is the centerpiece of the experience. But not all bracket interfaces are created equal. I recorded myself using six different bracket experiences — from a printed bracket to major sports platforms to fastbreak — and broke down the pros and cons of each.

## Takeaways

The best bracket experience depends on what you're looking for. If you want to fill out picks with friends, Yahoo's pool system is solid. If you want the official source of truth, the NCAA bracket is the way to go. If you want stats and context to understand the matchups, that's where fastbreak shines.

The biggest gap across all platforms is the lack of statistical depth at the point of decision. When you're staring at a first-round matchup trying to decide who advances, most bracket tools give you a seed number and a team name — fastbreak gives you the data to make an informed pick.
